Department Responsibilities and Resolution Timelines

Public Works Department handles road damage, potholes, footpath repairs, and bridge maintenance. Standard resolution time for pothole repairs is 7 working days from complaint registration. Major road damage may take up to 30 days depending on budget approval.

Electricity Department handles street light failures, damaged transformers, and exposed wiring hazards. Street light repairs are targeted within 3 working days. Any live wire or electrical hazard complaint is treated as an emergency and should receive a response within 24 hours.

Sanitation Department handles garbage collection, illegal dumping, and drainage cleaning. Illegal dumping complaints are inspected within 5 working days. Repeated dumping at the same location may result in fines to the responsible party once identified.

Water Board handles water leakage, pipeline bursts, and water quality complaints. Pipeline bursts are treated as emergencies with a target response time of same-day to 48 hours. Minor leakage complaints are resolved within 10 working days.

Vigilance Cell handles corruption complaints against government officials or departments. All corruption complaints are kept confidential. An initial inquiry is opened within 15 working days, and the citizen is not required to reveal their identity publicly.

General Administration handles complaints that do not fit the above categories and routes them to the appropriate department after initial review, typically within 3 working days.
